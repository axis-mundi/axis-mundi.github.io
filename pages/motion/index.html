Motion
Motion is a prototype developed to prove the concept of using discrete units to control continuous surface. The kinetic sculpture showcases a piece of smooth, loose fabric suspended in the air by sixteen actuating steel cables placed at a 4x4 grid. The matrix of servo motors, attached to one end of steel cables, continuously runs a sequence of unique compositions, displaying drastic spatial dimensions by tinkering with its control speed and amplitude. The seemingly still fabric is brought to life as one observes the seamless movement and tension of the fabric's physical form.
